How much money do you need to live in different European countries?

What is included in living expenses?

Costs for comfortable daily life starting from necessary as housing, food to personal wants as hotels, electronics, car, recreation. Rent prices are taken from the most expensive cities. If you can afford to cover all the expenses, you will have a comfortable life!

Living costs are assumed for 1 person, so accordingly 2 people should multiply two times.

Cost Distribution

Data Source: Eurostat

Rent takes most of the money

Countries with similar living expenses have similar costs distribution. In least expensive countries people spending proportionally more on rent and food. In more expensive countries people spending less on rent and more on health and personal wants. United Kingdom spends almost 1/2 of the budget for a rent, while Iceland spends 1/3 of the budget. Additionally, United Kingdom is expensive country, but has unique costs distribution comparing with the other expensive countries.

Conclusions

  • Regions have similar living expenses while people from more expensive countries can cover living expenses easier.

  • Some countries might have different phenomena as United Kingdom, where living expenses are high, but spending more on necessary things as rent, food.

  • People in more expensive countries spending proportionally more on personal wants, less on necessary.

  • A budget from 350k EUR allows to live in at least one country in a Europe for 20 years.

  • A budget from 800k EUR allows to live in South and East Europe for 20 years.
